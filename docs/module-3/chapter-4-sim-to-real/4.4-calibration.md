# 4.4 Calibration

## Introduction to Sim-to-Real Calibration

Calibration is a critical step in the sim-to-real transfer process, ensuring that simulation parameters accurately reflect real-world conditions. This chapter covers the systematic approaches to calibrate sensors, robot dynamics, and environmental parameters to minimize the reality gap between simulation and deployment.

### The Calibration Pipeline

The sim-to-real calibration process involves multiple interconnected calibration steps:

- **Intrinsic calibration**: Determining internal sensor parameters
- **Extrinsic calibration**: Establishing spatial relationships between sensors
- **Dynamic calibration**: Modeling robot physical properties and dynamics
- **Environmental calibration**: Matching simulation environment parameters to reality

## Sensor Calibration for Sim-to-Real Transfer

### Camera Calibration

Camera calibration ensures accurate mapping between simulated and real camera parameters:

- **Intrinsic parameters**: Focal length, principal point, distortion coefficients
- **Extrinsic parameters**: Position and orientation relative to robot frame
- **Temporal synchronization**: Ensuring proper timing between sensor readings
- **Validation procedures**: Testing calibration accuracy in real scenarios

### LiDAR Calibration

LiDAR sensors require specialized calibration for accurate simulation:

- **Beam alignment**: Correcting for misaligned laser beams
- **Range accuracy**: Calibrating distance measurements
- **Angular precision**: Ensuring accurate angular measurements
- **Noise modeling**: Characterizing real sensor noise patterns

### IMU Calibration

Inertial Measurement Unit calibration for accurate state estimation:

- **Bias correction**: Removing systematic sensor biases
- **Scale factor calibration**: Correcting for sensor scaling errors
- **Alignment calibration**: Aligning sensor axes with robot frame
- **Temperature compensation**: Accounting for temperature effects

## Robot Dynamic Calibration

### Mass and Inertia Properties

Accurately modeling robot mass distribution:

- **Mass measurement**: Determining component and total robot mass
- **Center of mass**: Calculating center of mass location
- **Inertia tensor**: Computing moments of inertia
- **Parameter identification**: Using system identification techniques

### Joint Dynamics Calibration

Calibrating joint-specific dynamic properties:

- **Friction modeling**: Characterizing static and dynamic friction
- **Viscous damping**: Measuring velocity-dependent damping
- **Actuator dynamics**: Modeling motor and transmission characteristics
- **Backlash compensation**: Accounting for mechanical play

### Contact and Friction Modeling

Modeling contact interactions for humanoid robots:

- **Foot-ground contact**: Modeling bipedal contact dynamics
- **Friction coefficients**: Measuring static and dynamic friction
- **Contact compliance**: Modeling soft contact behavior
- **Impact dynamics**: Characterizing collision responses

## Challenges and Limitations

### Environmental Factors

Environmental conditions affecting calibration:

- **Temperature variations**: Thermal effects on sensor and robot parameters
- **Humidity effects**: Environmental impact on sensor performance
- **Vibration and shock**: Dynamic environmental effects
- **Long-term drift**: Parameter changes over extended periods

### Computational Complexity

Calibration computational requirements:

- **Real-time constraints**: Performing calibration within system limits
- **Convergence guarantees**: Ensuring calibration algorithm convergence
- **Local minima**: Avoiding suboptimal calibration solutions
- **Multi-parameter optimization**: Handling high-dimensional parameter spaces

## Best Practices

### Systematic Calibration Approach

Recommended approaches for effective calibration:

- **Modular calibration**: Calibrating subsystems independently when possible
- **Iterative refinement**: Improving calibration through multiple iterations
- **Cross-validation**: Using separate data for calibration and validation
- **Documentation**: Maintaining detailed calibration records

### Safety Considerations

Ensuring safe calibration procedures:

- **Operational boundaries**: Maintaining safe robot operation during calibration
- **Emergency procedures**: Establishing calibration interruption protocols
- **Validation before deployment**: Verifying calibration quality before use
- **Monitoring systems**: Continuous assessment of calibration validity
