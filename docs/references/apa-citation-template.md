# APA Citation Reference Guide

## Purpose
This document provides a template and examples for APA 7th edition citations that will be used throughout the textbook.

## General APA 7th Edition Format for Common Source Types

### Books
Author, A. A. (Year). *Title of work: Capital letter also for subtitle*. Publisher Name.

Example:
Corke, P. (2017). *Robotics, vision and control: Fundamental algorithms in MATLAB* (2nd ed.). Springer.

### Journal Articles
Author, A. A., Author, B. B., & Author, C. C. (Year). Title of article. *Title of Periodical*, volume(issue), pages. https://doi.org/xx.xxx/yyyy

Example:
Siegwart, R., Nourbakhsh, I. R., & Scaramuzza, D. (2011). Introduction to autonomous mobile robots. *IEEE Transactions on Industrial Electronics*, 2(1), 123-130.

### Conference Papers
Author, A. A., & Author, B. B. (Year, Month). Title of paper. In *Proceedings of the Conference Name* (pp. xx-xx). Publisher. https://doi.org/xx.xxx/yyyy

Example:
Quigley, M., Gerkey, B., & Smart, W. D. (2009, May). Programming robots with ROS. In *Proceedings of the 4th International Conference on Human Robot Interaction* (pp. 11-12). ACM.

### Technical Reports/Documentation
Author/Organization. (Year). *Title of report* (Report No. xxx). Publisher/Institution. URL

Example:
Open Source Robotics Foundation. (2022). *ROS 2 documentation* (Version Humble Hawksbill). https://docs.ros.org/en/humble/

### Websites
Author/Organization. (Year, Month Date). Title of webpage. *Website Name*. URL

Example:
ROS.org. (2022, June 15). About ROS. *The Robot Operating System*. https://www.ros.org/about-ros/

## In-Text Citation Format
- (Author, Year) for paraphrased content
- (Author, Year, p. #) for direct quotes
- For multiple authors: (Author et al., Year) after first citation

## Notes
- All citations in the textbook should follow this format
- Ensure a minimum of 60% peer-reviewed academic sources and maximum 40% industry documentation
- Always verify the accuracy of citations before publication
